# Quality Gate

Run this before final delivery or major revision.

## Minimum Acceptance Standard

- Full report exceeds 20,000 Chinese characters unless the user requested otherwise.
- Report has a clear strategic main line, not only chapter-by-chapter aggregation.
- Key facts are sourced, user-provided, or marked as pending.
- Objectives are linked to situation analysis and deployment tasks.
- Risk responses and safeguards correspond to the actual risks and tasks.
- Language is central SOE formal style and free of casual phrasing.

## Structural Check

- [ ] Executive summary captures the whole report.
- [ ] Development foundation separates achievements, capabilities, and problems.
- [ ] Situation analysis produces implications for the group.
- [ ] Guiding thought, principles, and objectives are not repetitive.
- [ ] Business deployment has enough depth and is the longest section.
- [ ] Investment/value discussion distinguishes economic, strategic, social, and safety value.
- [ ] Risks are uncertainty-based, not duplicated weakness paragraphs.
- [ ] Safeguards are enabling mechanisms, not a second task list.

## Logic Check

Create a traceability table:

| Situation/Problem | Objective | Task | Safeguard | Risk Control |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Every important task should map back to a problem or opportunity. Every major target should have at least one task and one safeguard.

## Data Check

- [ ] Same indicator uses one unit and one source.
- [ ] Baseline year and target year are consistent.
- [ ] Forecasts identify assumptions.
- [ ] Tables have units and source notes.
- [ ] No exact number appears without source or placeholder.

## Style Check

Search and fix:

- "非常、极其、巨大、显著" without data
- "大力推进、持续强化、不断完善" without specific object and mechanism
- "可以说、基本上、这块、那个"
- repeated opening patterns across many paragraphs
- policy citations without enterprise implication

## Leadership-Readiness Check

Ask:

- Is the strategic choice clear?
- Does the report tell leadership what to decide?
- Are tradeoffs visible?
- Are difficult issues named professionally?
- Are implementation constraints realistic?
- Does the report protect the client from factual overclaiming?

## Final Delivery Note

At the end of a long report or revision, provide:

- estimated character count
- list of unresolved placeholders
- list of assumptions
- recommended next data inputs
- suggested next round: fact verification, leadership wording alignment, formatting/docx conversion
