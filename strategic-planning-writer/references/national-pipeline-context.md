## Strategic Department Perspective

The strategy department cares about:

- alignment with national strategy and SASAC central SOE requirements
- consistency with group-level strategy, business portfolio, and investment discipline
- medium/long-term strategic choices rather than only annual work tasks
- cross-business coordination: natural gas, crude oil, product oil, LNG, storage, digitalization, new energy, strategic emerging industries
- province and ministry coordination requirements
- measurable implementation paths and accountability mechanisms
- risk boundaries: safety, investment return, compliance, policy, market, technology, organization

Write with a board/leadership lens:

- What strategic choice is being made?
- What problem does this choice solve?
- Which capability must the group build?
- Which assets, resources, or institutional mechanisms support it?
- What should be done first, next, and later?

## National PipeChina Strategic Themes

Use these themes as applicable:

### National One-Network Operation

Discuss interconnection, unified dispatch, cross-regional resource allocation, pipeline capacity coordination, emergency supply, and efficient utilization of existing assets.

### Oil and Gas Market Reform

Use the "X+1+X" market structure carefully: multi-subject upstream supply, one national pipeline network platform, and multi-subject downstream sales/use. Explain how fair open access, transparent capacity allocation, and cost-reflective pipeline tariffs influence the group.

### Energy Security

Frame energy security in three layers:

1. resource access and diversified supply
2. infrastructure transport/storage/peak-shaving capability
3. safe, reliable, resilient operation under extreme scenarios

### Green and Low-Carbon Transition

Connect the pipeline network to the transition without overstating:

- natural gas as a transitional and flexible energy source where appropriate
- methane emission control, energy-saving operation, and low-carbon stations
- hydrogen, ammonia, methanol, CO2 pipelines, CCUS, underground space, and new energy integration as prospective opportunities requiring staged verification

### Digital and Intelligent Pipeline Network

Focus on business value:

- dispatch optimization
- predictive maintenance
- integrity management
- leak detection and emergency response
- asset lifecycle management
- capacity trading and customer service
- data governance and cyber security

Avoid treating "digitalization" as software procurement only.

## Sensitive Writing Boundaries

Do not:

- fabricate group internal strategy wording
- claim approval of a plan or project without evidence
- assign responsibilities to ministries, local governments, subsidiaries, or partners without user-provided basis
- disclose or infer confidential internal data
- overpromise strategic emerging businesses as mature profit engines before evidence supports it
- use aggressive commercial language inconsistent with central SOE public service and infrastructure platform roles
